Community Resources for Wilson's Disease

Updated 2024

People Who Can Help

There are lots of people who understand what you're going through and want to help:

  • Wilson Disease Association: A group that helps people all over the country
  • Online groups: Talk to other people who have Wilson's disease
  • Video chats: Meet people online to talk about Wilson's disease
  • Local meetups: Find people near you who understand
  • Email groups: Get updates and talk to others online

These groups can help you feel less alone and give you good advice about living with Wilson's disease.

Places to Get Help

There are special places that know how to help people with Wilson's disease:

  • University hospitals: Big hospitals that know a lot about rare diseases
  • Liver centers: Places that help with liver problems
  • Brain centers: Places that help with brain problems
  • Gene centers: Places that check your genes
  • Rare disease centers: Places that help with rare conditions

These places can help you get the best care for Wilson's disease.

Help Paying for Treatment

There are ways to get help paying for your medical care:

  • Drug companies: Programs to help pay for medicine
  • Hospital help: Money to help with medical bills
  • Local help: People in your town who want to help
  • Religious groups: Help with medical and living costs
  • Community help: Money for emergencies

Ask your doctor or the Wilson Disease Association about ways to get help paying for treatment.

Groups That Help

These groups work hard to help people with Wilson's disease:

  • Wilson Disease Association: The biggest group just for Wilson's disease
  • American Liver Foundation: Helps with all kinds of liver problems
  • National Organization for Rare Disorders: Helps with all rare diseases
  • Genetic Alliance: Works on genetic conditions like Wilson's
  • Global Genes: Connects people with rare diseases all over the world

These groups talk to the government, help pay for research, and teach people about Wilson's disease. They're your voice when you need one.

Helpful Stuff Online

The internet has tons of great info about Wilson's disease:

  • Learning about it: Websites with easy-to-understand info
  • Finding doctors: Directories of doctors who know about Wilson's
  • Talking to others: Forums where you can share stories and ask questions
  • Watching videos: People sharing their experiences
  • Phone apps: Help tracking medicine and symptoms

Just make sure you're getting your info from trustworthy places. The Wilson Disease Association website is always a good place to start.

Stuff to Read and Watch

Want to learn more about Wilson's disease? Here's where to look:

  • Easy guides: Simple fact sheets about Wilson's disease
  • Books: Stories from other people with Wilson's
  • Podcasts: Listen to experts talk about Wilson's disease
  • Medical articles: If you want the science-y details
  • Newsletters: Get updates about new treatments and research

The Wilson Disease Association can send you free info packets if you don't like looking things up online.

Helping Find New Treatments

You can help doctors find better treatments for Wilson's disease:

  • Clinical trials: Try new treatments before they're available to everyone
  • Surveys: Share your experience with the disease
  • Patient registries: Add your info to help researchers see patterns
  • Biobanks: Donate samples to help with research
  • Patient advisory boards: Tell researchers what matters to you

Being part of research isn't for everyone, but it's one way to feel like you're fighting back against the disease.

Real People, Real Stories

Hearing from other people with Wilson's disease can help a lot:

"When I was diagnosed at 16, I was terrified. But here I am, 20 years later, married with kids and working full time. The medicine works! You just have to take it every day."

Sarah, diagnosed at 16

"The hardest part was convincing my teenage son to take his medicine every day. Once he met other kids with Wilson's at a conference, he finally got it. Now he manages everything himself."

Michael, father of a son with Wilson's

"I was having trouble with my hands shaking, but my doctor adjusted my medicine and now it's much better. Don't give up if the first treatment doesn't work perfectly!"

Elena, living with Wilson's for 10 years

Want to share your story? The Wilson Disease Association is always looking for more stories to help newly diagnosed people.
