Genetic Counselor's Report: Wilson's Disease

Updated 2024

Inheritance Pattern

Wilson's disease is an autosomal recessive genetic disorder, meaning:

  • Inheritance pattern: Autosomal recessive (requires two copies of the defective gene)
  • Probability of passing: 25% chance if both parents are carriers
  • Carrier status: 50% chance of being a carrier if one parent has the disease
  • Gene location: ATP7B gene on chromosome 13
  • Genetic testing: Available for diagnosis and carrier screening

Understanding the inheritance pattern is crucial for family planning and genetic counseling for affected individuals and their families.

Punnett Square

A Punnett square illustrates the inheritance pattern of Wilson's disease:

W (normal) w (Wilson's)
W (normal) WW (normal) Ww (carrier)
w (Wilson's) Ww (carrier) ww (affected)

This Punnett square shows that when both parents are carriers (Ww), there is:

  • 25% chance of having an affected child (ww)
  • 50% chance of having a carrier child (Ww)
  • 25% chance of having a normal child (WW)

This detailed pedigree illustrates how Wilson's disease can be passed through multiple generations, showing:

  • Carrier parents having children with different statuses (affected, carrier, or normal)
  • Affected individuals emerging when both parents are carriers or when a carrier and affected individual have children
  • Carrier status that can silently continue through generations without affected individuals
  • Generation skipping - affected individuals appearing in Generation II, then again in Generation IV
  • Equal gender distribution - Wilson's disease affects males and females equally

Understanding this inheritance pattern is crucial for family planning and genetic counseling for families with a history of Wilson's disease.

Genetic Testing

Genetic testing for Wilson's disease involves:

  • ATP7B gene sequencing: Identifies specific mutations
  • Carrier testing: For family members of affected individuals
  • Prenatal testing: Available for at-risk pregnancies
  • Newborn screening: Not routinely performed but available
  • Diagnostic confirmation: For individuals with symptoms

Genetic counseling is recommended before and after testing to help individuals understand the implications of test results.

Family Planning Considerations

Family planning considerations for individuals with Wilson's disease or carriers include:

  • Reproductive options: Understanding risks and alternatives
  • Preimplantation genetic diagnosis: Available for some couples
  • Prenatal testing: Options for at-risk pregnancies
  • Family history: Importance of genetic testing for relatives
  • Genetic counseling: Support for decision-making

These considerations help individuals make informed decisions about family planning.
